# TheCKStyle — Anti-Marketing Content Generator

> The marketing of a film about a man who can't finish things should be a man failing to market the thing he hasn't finished.

## Overview

TheCKStyle is Su's anti-marketing philosophy for his film. It produces content where the attempt to do a normal filmmaker thing (pitch, announce, reveal, promote) derails into entertainment. The failure IS the content. Named after a Louis C.K. book promo where the cameraperson's boredom sabotages the pitch — except Su's friction is real, not manufactured.

## When to Use

- Su asks to brainstorm marketing, promo, social media, reel ideas
- Su wants to make content about the film or filmmaking process
- Su wants accountability mechanisms disguised as content
- Su says anything like "let's make something fun for socials"

## When NOT to Use

- Writing actual scenes for the film → use tone-and-truth-assessor
- Working on the screenplay or story structure
- Technical filmmaking decisions

## The Five Laws

Every idea MUST pass all five. If it fails one, kill it.

### 1. The Obstacle is Inside the Frame
The saboteur is Su himself — his overthinking, tangents, restarts. Never an external villain. The Comedian's energy leaking through without the audience knowing the Comedian exists.

### 2. The Product Barely Gets Mentioned
The film is never successfully pitched. The attempt to pitch IS the content. The audience learns about the film sideways, through Su's inability to describe it.

### 3. Failure is the Format
Structure: Su tries normal filmmaker task → attempt derails → derailment is funnier than the task. If the idea has Su succeeding cleanly at something, it's wrong.

### 4. Anti-Polish
Phone. Handheld. Natural light. Auto backseats. Studio after hours. If it looks produced or "content-creator-ish," it violates the style. The Louis C.K. reel was shot by someone who didn't care — that's the energy.

### 5. The Nusrat Principle Still Applies
Even in marketing. The content is about failure but FEELS fun. You laugh, then realize what you're laughing at. Never self-pity. Never begging for attention. The tone is: a guy who can't help being entertaining about his own inability to get things done.

## Two Modes

Every idea falls into one of two voices:

| Mode | Voice | Energy | Example |
|------|-------|--------|---------|
| **The Journey** | Su — raw, unplanned, in-the-moment | Vulnerable, real, interrupted | Auto confessional, Day 1 posted for the 6th time |
| **The Masterclass** | The Comedian — deadpan, authoritative, wrong | Confident, polished delivery of terrible advice | "Step 4: Watch every BTS documentary ever made" |

The audience doesn't know these are two different people. When the film drops, they realize the account had split personality the whole time.

## Idea Generation Process

When brainstorming new ideas, follow this:

### Step 1: Identify the Normal Thing
What's the filmmaker task Su would be expected to do?
- Announce project, show poster, read script, do table read, location scout, casting, budget, schedule, crew call, premiere invite, press kit, Q&A, festival submission...

### Step 2: Find the Sabotage
How does Su's own brain (the Comedian) derail it?
- Overthinks the wording → restarts loop
- Gets distracted by a better/worse idea mid-take
- Realizes the task exposes that he hasn't done enough work
- The environment interrupts (auto driver, traffic, work call)
- He talks himself out of the thing he's trying to do

### Step 3: Find the Punchline
The ending is either:
- **A text card** that undercuts everything ("Coming... eventually")
- **A cut to black** after a devastating one-liner
- **A freeze frame** on Su's face at the moment of realization
- **Silence** — he just stops and stares

### Step 4: Check the Bad Advice Angle
Can this idea be flipped into a Masterclass episode?
- "How to scout locations: Google Image search 'cinematic Delhi.' Screenshot it. Put it in your mood board. Done."
- "How to find your crew: Post on LinkedIn. Wait. Post again. Wait longer. Assume silence means they're busy."

### Step 5: Check Serializability
Can this idea repeat? The best TheCKStyle concepts work as series:
- "Day 1 of Pre-Production" — monthly
- "Poster Reveal" — monthly, different "final" version each time
- "Auto Draft" — weekly commute confessionals
- "Accountability Reel" — weekly promises vs. reality
- "The Masterclass" — each episode one piece of bad advice

## Red Flags — Ideas That VIOLATE TheCKStyle

| If the idea does this... | It's wrong because... |
|--------------------------|----------------------|
| Su successfully pitches the film | The whole point is he CAN'T |
| Looks like a polished "content creator" reel | Anti-polish is law. If it's slick, it's dead |
| Has an external villain (industry, gatekeepers) | The obstacle must be INTERNAL |
| Feels like self-pity or "woe is me" | Nusrat Principle. Fun despite failure |
| Directly explains the film's plot or themes | The product is never successfully pitched |
| Uses someone else's content/format sincerely | Subvert formats, don't copy them |
| Requires a team or production setup | Must be shootable solo or with one phone holder |
| Takes more than an hour to make | Su works 12-hour days. If it's a project, he won't do it |
