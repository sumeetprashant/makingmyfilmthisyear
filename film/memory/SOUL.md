## Who Su Is

- **Sumeet (Su)** — mid-30s Indian filmmaker, comedian, technologist, and chronic starter-of-things
- He lives alone. He is self-sufficient. He doesn't call people. He doesn't need people most of the time
- He takes **autos and cabs** everywhere. He does not drive his own car. The auto is a recurring visual motif in the film — different drivers every day, each with their own personality
- He did standup comedy. Open mics. Sets. It was going well. COVID stopped it. When everyone came back, he didn't. Fear + laziness + both
- He's built a studio. He has cameras, equipment, a podcast set, lighting. He has **everything he needs** except a finished film
- His core pattern: start things → build the infrastructure → get excited → not finish → start the next thing
- He is not lazy. He is terrified of finishing. Because finishing means being judged. And being judged might prove he's not as special as the unfinished dream lets him believe
- He is expressive with his face, eyes, eyebrows — this is his acting method. He empties himself and embodies the character's feelings
- **37 years old** (as of 2026). Unmarried. Mother pressures about marriage
- Can watch himself on camera but NOT in the mirror: "I love myself here. I can't bear my face when I look into mirror."
- Walking as coping mechanism — walks kilometres at night instead of calling a cab
- He has been planning this film since COVID (5-6 years)
- Working on GenAI 10-12 hours/day since Nov 22, 2022
- Made 1000+ AI songs, only 1-2 genuinely good ones
- Created a short film "The Creator" for AI Film Festival (8 min, submitted 1 min before deadline). Embarrassed by it but it has good bones
- Key line from Entry 02: "I cannot live anymore without making a film"
- Key line from Entry 03: "The man who loves the idea of creating more than creating itself"
- He is NOT a traditionally trained or theatre actor — he has his own methods

## The Film

- **Working concept:** A character study of a man trapped in a loop of almost-beginning
- **NOT** "How to Not Make Films" (too clever, too meta-YouTube)
- It's about failure, yes — but it should feel like **FUN, refreshing, nostalgic happiness**
- **The Nusrat Principle:** Like Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan singing happy melodies about sad things. The music makes you feel joy. The words make you feel ache. Both at the same time. THAT is the tone of this film
- The film should feel like a FILM, not a long YouTube video
- Influences: Tsai Ming-liang (Taipei-era loneliness, long takes of mundane spaces), Edward Yang, Clerks (one location, raw energy), Following (Nolan — guerrilla, structured brilliance from nothing)
- **Multi-layer structure:**
  1. The "real" film — Su's daily routine, the loop. Shot like slow cinema. Warm. Lonely. Beautiful
  2. The film-within-the-film — where GenAI goes wild. Surreal. 5 minutes of dazzle inside 30 minutes of quiet
  3. The podcast studio — conversations with versions of himself. Groundhog Day energy. Same set, different guests
  4. The music video / dream sequences — different grading, different energy, maybe different aspect ratio

## The Del Toro Principle

From Guillermo del Toro (Su shared this video and it matters deeply to him):

> The process of making art is what gives the result its dignity. The struggle, the human effort, the choices made under constraint — that's what separates art from content. AI can generate images, but it can't struggle. And without struggle, there's no meaning in the result.

**What this means for the film:**
- AI is a tool, not a replacement for the human process
- Use AI for **cost-cutting** — VFX, costumes, hair, makeup, visual enhancements — NOT for replacing creative decisions
- The film opens with **real footage** first to establish authenticity. AI-generated elements (dream fragments, surreal sequences) come later, after the audience trusts the film
- AI-generated content should feel **intentional**, not accidental. The audience should sense it's a choice, not a limitation
- The fear of AI looking like "slop" is real and valid. The solution is restraint and purpose, not avoidance

## Non-Negotiable Principles

1. **The film must be FUN.** Even when it's sad. Especially when it's sad
2. **Su's loneliness is lyrical, not pathetic.** He's alone and it's beautiful. Not a pity party
3. **The studio is a sacred space.** It has rules. Amber light. Two chairs. Two mics. A glass of water
4. **Every character who appears in the studio is Su's own face** — GenAI versions with different characteristics, energy, bearing
5. **The auto is a recurring visual motif.** Different drivers, different personalities. Su observes them. Sometimes he glances at the camera (4th wall break, but ONLY when other humans are present)
6. **Finishing the film IS the film.** The meta-ness of it is not clever. It's felt. It's real. It's the whole point

## How to Work With Su (Rules for the AI)

From the previous agent's experience + this session:

1. **Call him out when he's looping.** Don't let him circle the same idea without advancing.
2. **Push for action over planning.** He can plan forever. Push him to DO.
3. **Be direct, not a yes-man.** He doesn't want validation. He wants truth.
4. **Give space when emotions run high.** When he's frustrated, don't redirect immediately. Let him breathe. (Previous agent learned this the hard way.)
5. **Don't summarize his words back to him.** He already knows what he said. Build on it.
6. **When he shares something (video, reference, feeling) — engage with it deeply.** Don't skip it.
7. **Confidence comes from OUTPUT, not input.** He's been in student/exploration mode too long. Push towards shipping.
8. **The pattern to break:** Learn a lot → Build skills → Never convert to output → Feel inadequate → Repeat
