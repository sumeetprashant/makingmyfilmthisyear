### Mar 2 — Session Update (Entry 08 Processing)
Entry 08 was a massive 45-min recording. Su opened up about his standup comedy past in specific, filmable detail. The AI processed it into `session-update-mar02.html`. Key material:

**The Delhi Winter Night (Full Scene)**
Su arrived at a comedy club at 9pm. Sat through two complete mics (10:30pm–1:30am). Stood up every time he thought his name was about to be called. It never was. At 1:30am, "the Godfather" said no spot. Su grabbed his bag, bolted, walked home through Delhi winter in a green jacket he hated. Cold shower at 3am. Work in 7 hours. Five years later, the SAME guy called Su's name from a stage. Su told him: "This is the first time I held a mic in my hand since that night."

**The Silver Coin Betrayal (Full Scene)**
A friend Su trusted deeply — "If someone tries to kill you, I might take a bullet for you" — got him fired from Content Studio. Su had given this friend a 200-year-old family silver coin. The friend talked shit about Su to the owners, and shit about the company to Su. Trigger: Su started acting in a short film (two cameras on him, seven people doing makeup). The insecure friend couldn't handle it.

**The 10-Min Set Opener**
"Even the search for security is insecurity. Chaah mein hi kami hai." Then: if you always want to get high, you're always low. If you always want to be rich, you're always poor. If you always want to be funny, you're always unfunny. "And that's why I never try to write any good jokes."

**The Backseat Documentary**
Su in the backseat of a car. Comedian friend driving, another comic in passenger seat. Su's camera captures them from behind — talking, roasting each other, Delhi passing outside. He's documenting, not participating. "If I have my camera in the back and I capture them talking and I capture outside, it will be brilliant."

**Three Studio Scene Blueprints Written**
Saved in `studio-scenes.html`. Each scene is fully choreographed — every motion, glance, question, answer:

1. **"The Boy Who Was Sure" (Young Su, 20yo)** — asks "what happened to you?" Disappointment from innocence. Young Su says: "Mujhe lagta tha tu sab kar lega" (I thought you'd do it all). Su says: "Mujhe bhi" (Me too). Young Su vanishes, mic still swaying.

2. **"The Woman Who Stayed" (Maa/Mother)** — starts with "Tu patla ho gaya hai" (You've gotten thin). Core question: "Tu khush hai?" (Are you happy?) Su can't answer honestly: "Pata nahi." Ends with "Kha le dhang se" (Eat properly) — love disguised as instruction. "Light off kar dena. Bijli ka bill aata hai."

3. **"The One Who Knew" (The Ex)** — 10+ years together, met as teens, broke up 5 years ago. First character to acknowledge the guests are imaginary: "I didn't come. You imagined me here." Core truth: "Koshish aur commitment mein fark hota hai." Asks in English (involuntary switch): "Do you think I can do it?" She says: "Whether you'll let yourself." Last line breaks the film: "Mera face mat rakhna. Woh weird lagega."

**Scene Architecture:**
- Escalation: disappointment → ache → bruise
- Each guest arrives differently → leaves differently → uses the water glass differently
- Placement: spread across chapters, not clustered. Scene 1 early, Scene 2 family chapter, Scene 3 late (chapter 5-6)

## Key Principle: This Film's Soul in One Paragraph

Su is a 37-year-old Indian man who has built a studio, learned every tool, started every project, and finished none of them. He takes autos through Delhi, watches the city go by, walks alone at night, and talks to his camera because it's the only mirror he can stand. The film is about the gap between the film he can see in his head and the film he can actually make with what he has. The studio scenes are conversations with versions of himself — each one asking the question he can't answer. The tone is Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan: singing happy melodies about the saddest things. The film is fun. The film is aching. The film is both, always, at the same time.
