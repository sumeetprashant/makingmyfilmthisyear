# Topic: Characters

> All characters in the film and their roles.

---

## Su (Sumeet) — The Protagonist

- Mid-30s. Indian. Lives alone in Delhi
- Takes autos and cabs everywhere (NO car). The auto ride is a recurring visual element
- Former standup comedian — stopped after COVID, never went back (fear + laziness + both)
- Has built a full studio: cameras, podcast set, equipment, lights. Has everything. Except a finished film
- Pattern: starts things → builds infrastructure → gets excited → doesn't finish → starts next thing
- Self-sufficient. Doesn't call people. Doesn't need people most of the time
- Expressive face — acts through eyes, eyebrows, microexpressions. Not traditionally trained. Empties himself and embodies the character
- Performs in Hindi. Breaks in English (involuntary language switch in vulnerable moments)
- Wears oversized shirts, jeans that gave up on being formal
- Walking as coping mechanism — late night walks, walking for kilometres instead of calling a cab

## Studio Guests (GenAI versions of Su)

### Young Su (20 years old)
- Straight posture. Unweathered. Eyes wider. Hair shorter. T-shirt that still fits
- He hasn't learned to slouch yet. Posture of someone who doesn't know failure as texture
- Asks direct questions without protecting feelings — the 20-year-old way
- His disappointment is quiet: "Mujhe lagta tha tu sab kar lega" (I thought you'd do it all)

### Maa (Mother)
- Su's face with dupatta. Softer jaw. Slower eyes. Hands folded in lap
- The diagnostic look — reading his face since birth
- Starts with "Tu patla ho gaya hai" (You've gotten thin) — practical love first
- Core question: "Tu khush hai?" (Are you happy?) — the simplest, hardest question
- Exits with: "Kha le dhang se" (Eat properly) — love disguised as instruction

### The Ex
- Su's face with feminine energy. Longer hair maybe. Different stillness
- Together 10+ years, met as teenagers, broke up 5 years ago
- She's the only human witness to the complete pattern — first open mic through every unfinished thing
- Precise, not cruel: "Koshish aur commitment mein fark hota hai"
- First character to acknowledge the studio guests are imaginary: "I didn't come. You imagined me here"
- Last line breaks the film's own conceit: "Mera face mat rakhna. Woh weird lagega"

## Non-Studio Characters

### The Germany Friend — POTENTIAL (not written yet)
- Real childhood friend. Went to school together
- Reconnected during Su's Europe backpacking trip. Lived with him for weeks
- Now settled in Germany. Married. Planning kids
- They have drinks on video calls every few months
- Su went to his wedding
- Would appear via VIDEO CALL, not in the studio — Su doesn't call people, so the friend probably calls him
- Purpose: reveals Su's interpersonal side. Shows how he is with someone who knows him and keeps him despite his shortcomings
- NOT a podcast guest. A real friend. Different mechanism

### Auto Drivers — RECURRING (not scripted individually)
- Different driver every day
- Each has a personality: respectful, disrespectful, in a hurry, lazy, young, old
- Su observes them silently → 4th wall glance to camera
- Visual motif, not dialogue scenes
