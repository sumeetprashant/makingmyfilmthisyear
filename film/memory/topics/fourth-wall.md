# Topic: 4th Wall Break

> Rules for when Su does and doesn't break the 4th wall.

---

## The Rule (LOCKED)

**Su can break the 4th wall (glance at camera) ONLY when other humans are present.**

### When he CAN glance at the camera:
- In autos, reacting to the driver's behavior (staring at girls, breaking signals, being rude/kind)
- In the studio, when a guest says something that lands
- During any scene where another person is physically present
- These glances function as comedy and connection — Su sharing a moment with the audience

### When he CANNOT break the 4th wall:
- When he is **alone** — walking at night, in his room, on the terrace, in contemplative moments
- These solo scenes are the "lyrical loneliness" — the camera observes him like a documentary, and he is unaware of being watched
- Breaking the 4th wall in these moments would destroy the vulnerability and the Tsai Ming-liang quality

## Why This Works

- Creates two distinct modes: **public Su** (aware, comedic, sharing) vs **private Su** (unaware, vulnerable, alone)
- Su's face and eyes ARE his acting instrument — the glances put that to use
- The auto scenes become a recurring motif: different driver every day, Su's reaction to camera tells the story
- The contrast between observed/unobserved Su is what makes the character feel real

## The Auto Driver Example (from Su)

> "Yesterday I was in an auto and this old driver was staring at girls half his age. I was staring at him in horror. A quick glance to camera at that moment tells the viewers everything without any dialogue. And every day these drivers are different — some respectful, some disrespectful, some in a hurry, some lazy, some break signals, some follow rules."

This is the model for how the 4th wall break works: **observation + reaction + glance.**
