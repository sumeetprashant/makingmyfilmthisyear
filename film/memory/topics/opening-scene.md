# Topic: Opening Scene

> The film's opening. Still being decided.

---

## Status: UNDECIDED 🔓

Su is divided. Two approaches have been discussed. Neither has been locked.

## Option A: 4th Wall Based Opening

- Su addresses the camera directly at the start
- Sets up the film's self-aware quality right away
- Connected to the 44th wall (4th wall) approach
- Concern: might set a tone that conflicts with the lyrical loneliness scenes later

## Option B: Versions of Su Rehearsing Their Lines

- Multiple versions of Su standing in the frame, each rehearsing lines from films that were never made (or maybe some were made)
- Like actors at an audition — **focused, reading their lines, preparing for roles** 
- They are NOT distracted. NOT yawning. NOT bored. They are busy, engaged, like someone who showed up for an audition (⚠️ the AI incorrectly described them as "distracted" — see CORRECTIONS.md)
- Sets up the multi-version concept visually before explaining it
- Some may be from films that were actually completed in Su's past
- **Concern:** If using GenAI to change clothes/hair/makeup for these versions, it might look too obviously AI-generated

## What Su Said About the Opening

> "First it was coming when we decided to keep the 4th wall. It was a 4th wall based opening. Then when you asked me not to go into 4th wall, then different versions of me standing rehearsing their lines practicing their roles for the films that were never made fully... or maybe some of them were made."

## Key Constraint

- If AI is used to generate the different character versions in the opening, the quality needs to be convincing enough to not break immersion in the very first scene
- Opening sets the audience's expectations — if it looks "AI-generated" in a distracting way, the audience may not trust the rest of the film

## Notes

- This decision is blocked by the broader question of how AI-generated visuals will look in the final film
- The opening needs to match the Nusrat Principle — fun, intriguing, pulling you in with warmth, not with a hook
