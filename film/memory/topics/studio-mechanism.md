# Topic: Studio Mechanism

> How the podcast studio works in the film. Rules, guests, visual language.

---

## The Studio

- **Location:** Su's own podcast studio. Real. Physical. He built it
- **Lighting:** Amber. Warm. Consistent across all studio scenes
- **Setup:** Two chairs facing each other. Two mics on stands. A glass of water on Su's side
- **Camera:** Fly on the wall. Fixed position. The audience is eavesdropping, not being addressed
- **Su's position:** Always on the left
- **Guest's position:** Always on the right

## The Guests

All studio guests are GenAI versions of Su — same face, modified with AI to carry different characteristics, energy, and bearing. They are NOT real people. They are projections of Su's internal world.

### Confirmed Guests (scenes written in `studio-scenes.html`):
1. **Young Su (20 years old)** — "The Boy Who Was Sure" — straight posture, unweathered, still certain about everything. Asks "what happened to you?"
2. **Maa (Mother)** — "The Woman Who Stayed" — Su's face with dupatta, maternal bearing, diagnostic eyes. Asks "are you happy?"
3. **The Ex** — "The One Who Knew" — Su's face with feminine energy, together 10+ years since teens, broke up 5 years ago. Asks "will you let yourself?"

## Scene Architecture

- Scenes escalate in emotional weight (disappointment → ache → bruise)
- Each guest ARRIVES differently (Young Su appears suddenly → Maa is already sitting → The Ex develops like a photograph)
- Each guest LEAVES differently (Young Su stands and walks → Maa gives instruction and exits → The Ex vanishes)
- Each uses the **glass of water** differently (Su fidgets → Maa puts hand on his hand holding it → Su doesn't touch it)
- These physical threads give shared language while making each scene distinct

## Distribution

- Studio scenes should be SPREAD across different chapters, NOT consecutive
- Scene 1 (Young Su) → Chapter 1 or 2
- Scene 2 (Maa) → wherever the family chapter is
- Scene 3 (The Ex) → late chapter, maybe 5 or 6, when audience has full context
- Conversations should get longer as the film progresses
