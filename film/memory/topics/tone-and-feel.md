# Topic: Tone and Feel

> What the film FEELS like. The emotional register. The aesthetic compass.

---

## The Nusrat Principle (CORE)

Named after Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan — the Qawwali master who sings happy melodies about devastatingly sad things. His music makes you feel joy in your body while his words make you feel ache in your chest. Both at the same time.

**This film works the same way.**

- The subject is failure, loneliness, not finishing things
- The FEELING is fun, warmth, nostalgia, a refreshing kind of happiness
- The audience should smile through the sadness — not feel beaten down by it

## What the Tone IS

- **FUN.** Above all else. If a scene isn't fun to watch, it needs to be reworked
- **Refreshing.** Not another depressing indie film about a sad man. This is NEW
- **Nostalgic happiness.** The feeling of remembering something that hurt but makes you smile now
- **Lyrical.** Not confessional. Not YouTube-diary. LYRICAL — poetic, visual, atmospheric
- **Warm.** Even the loneliest scenes feel warm — amber light, familiar spaces, a man who is broken but not cold

## What the Tone is NOT

- **NOT a pity party.** Su is not asking for sympathy. He's sharing a pattern
- **NOT depressing.** The film has failures in it. It is not ABOUT being depressed
- **NOT a YouTube video.** This is a FILM. Shot like one. Paced like one. The terrace scene, the auto rides, the studio — these are cinema
- **NOT ironic or clever-for-the-sake-of-clever.** The meta quality (a film about not making films) is not a gimmick. It's felt, not performed

## Visual Tone

- **Amber, warm, golden lighting** — especially in the studio
- **Delhi atmosphere** — smog, late-night streets, auto rides through traffic
- **Mundane spaces made poetic through framing** (Tsai Ming-liang influence)
- **Silence that says more than dialogue** — long takes, held shots, stillness
- **Aspect ratio shifts possible** for dream/music video sequences — different worlds, different frames

## Emotional Architecture

The film moves through emotional registers:

1. **Fun/light** — the auto rides, the comedic observations, 4th wall glances
2. **Wistful/nostalgic** — the studio conversations, memories of what could have been
3. **Aching/intimate** — the mother scene, the ex scene, the moments alone at night
4. **Beautiful/lyrical** — the film-within-the-film, dream sequences, music moments

These registers alternate and layer. The film never stays in one mode too long.

## Reference Points

| Reference | What to take from it |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan | Joy and pain simultaneously. Happy melody, heartbreaking lyrics |
| Tsai Ming-liang | Loneliness in occupied spaces. Long takes. Mundane beauty |
| Edward Yang | Family, memory, the weight of unlived lives |
| Clerks | One location, real talk, no money, raw energy |
| Following (Nolan) | Structured brilliance from nothing. Guerrilla filmmaking |
